             Jose P. Rizal

                                                    1861 – 1896

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born on June 19, 1861 in Calamba, Laguna. Born a genius, he was a doctor, writer, printer, engineer, linguist, scientist, sculptor and philanthropist. Many interesting stories and anecdotes were written about our national hero. One well-known anecdote which happened when he was a grade schooler goes this way: Pepe (Rizal's nickname) and his friends were sailing over Laguna de Bay to attend a nearby town fiesta. They were all very happy. Pepe was impressed and thrilled by the beauty of the water and began playing and touching the water with his hand. Suddenly, one of his slippers fell into the water.

After a few minutes, to the great surprise of his friends, Pepe took his other slipper and threw it into the water. His friends asked him why not recover the fallen slipper instead of throwing the remaining one. Pepe replied very calmly, "My slipper is useless without the other. Someday, a poor fisherman will find them both." Jose Rizal came from a rich family in Calamba, Laguna. He studied at Ateneo University and at Univeristy of Santo Tomas then University of Madrid in Spain.

He finished medicine, and was a well-known linguist in different languages. But the greatest achievements that Rizal accomplished were during the time of the Propaganda Movement. He sought reforms during the Spanish government through his novels "Noli Me Tangere" (1887) and "El Filibusterismo" (1891). These books exposed Spanish cruelty to his countrymen. They spread worldwide and awakened nationalism. Rizal was sentenced to death on December 31, 1896 at Bagumbayan, now Luneta Park. Truly, Doctor Jose Rizal is the pride of the Filipino.

WATCH & LEARN



  • Jose Rizal could speak and write in more than 20 languages, including Filipino, Spanish, German, French, English, Latin, Greek, Arabic, Malay, Hebrew, Sanskrit, Dutch, Catalan, Italian, Chinese, Japanese, Portuguese, Swedish, Russian, and Tagalog dialects? He was also a skilled sculptor, painter, and poet even before he became a doctor.
  • Rizal completed his novel "Noli Me Tangere" at just 26 years old while living in Berlin, Germany. On the night before his execution, he wrote his farewell poem "Mi Ultimo Adios" which is considered one of the greatest pieces of Philippine literature, and hid it inside an alcohol stove which he gave to his family.
