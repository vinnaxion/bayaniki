        Jose Abad Santos

                                                    1886 - 1942

On February 19, 1886, Jose Abad Santos y Basco, fifth Chief Justice of the Supreme Court, was born in San Fernando, Pampanga.

Abad Santos was known to lead a modest life; he abstained from alcohol, gambling, and smoking. He declined presents in order to stay clear of any corruption. He was never engaged in a scandal during his service in administration. He didn't become wealthy while in office either.

Shortly after the start of World War II, on December 24, 1941, Abad Santos was appointed Chief Justice. He traveled with the Commonwealth government to Corregidor, where on December 30, 1941, he pledged to President Manuel L. Quezon and Vice President Sergio Osmeña for their second terms.

In order to keep the Commonwealth government money out of the hands of the enemy, he and Manuel Roxas also agreed to oversee its destruction.

Chief Justice Abad Santos had the option to accompany President Quezon when he departed for the United States via Australia. However, he chose to stay in the Philippines.

He was named government caretaker by President Quezon, who gave him complete authority to act on behalf of the Philippine President in territories that the Japanese had not yet invaded.

However, the Japanese soldiers captured him and his son Pepito in Cebu. The Japanese wanted Abad Santos to swear allegiance to the Japanese flag.

"I would rather die than be a traitor," he said bravely to his captors. "I would prefer to die rather than live in shame."

Although he had nothing to do with military operations, they imputed to him the destruction of bridges and other public works in Cebu.

Because of his consistent refusal to cooperate with the enemy, the Japanese sentenced him to death. He was brought to Malabang, Lanao, together with son Pepito.

Before he was shot to death on May 2, 1942, he was able to talk to his son Jose Jr. His last parting words to his son were:

"Do not cry, Pepito, show these people that you are brave. It is an honor to die for one's country." Not everybody has that chance".

WATCH & LEARN



  • Jose Abad Santos was the fifth Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines and the first Filipino to serve in that position during the Commonwealth period.
  • During World War II, he refused to cooperate with the Japanese forces and chose to remain loyal to the Philippines and the United States, even though he knew it could cost him his life.
  • Before his execution in 1942, he told his son, “Do not cry, Pepito. Show these people that you are brave,” which became one of the most remembered statements of courage in Philippine history.
