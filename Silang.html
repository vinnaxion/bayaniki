             Gabriela Silang

                                                    1731 - 1763

Gabriela Silang was the wife of Diego Silang, a revolutionary leader of Ilocos who was captured and killed by the Spaniards. After the death of her husband, the Spanish Army invaded the camp and seized all firearms, ammunition, and important documents that Diego Silang had left behind. Faced with this great loss, Gabriela did not give up hope. Instead, she chose to continue the fight that her husband had started for the freedom of the Ilocano people. She declared that it was necessary for her to carry on the freedom that Diego Silang had begun, showing a determination and courage that inspired those around her to keep fighting even in the face of overwhelming odds.

Gabriela Silang and the rest of her troops continued to wage battle against the Spaniards. However, their insufficient arms eventually led to their defeat. Despite the bravery of her soldiers and her own fierce leadership, the lack of weapons and supplies made it impossible to sustain the fight against the better-equipped Spanish forces. This was one of the great hardships faced by Filipino revolutionaries during this period — not a lack of will, but a lack of resources to match the strength of their colonial oppressors.

Gabriela Silang was eventually captured in the mountains of Abra. The Spaniards brought her and her remaining companions back to Ilocos Sur, where she was tried and sentenced to death. On September 20, 1763, Gabriela Silang and the rest of her revolutionary troops were executed in Vigan, Ilocos Sur. She faced her end as she had lived — with courage and an unwavering love for her people. She is remembered today as the Revolutionary Woman General of the Philippines, the first Filipino woman to lead an armed uprising against colonial rule.

WATCH & LEARN



  • Gabriela Silang was skilled in horseback riding and was known to wield a bolo sword in battle, a striking image that became iconic in Philippine history.
  • An asteroid discovered in 1993 was named "7026 Gabrielasilang" in her honor. The women's organization GABRIELA, founded in 1984, was named after her as a tribute to her bravery.
  • She was also among the nine Filipinos recommended as national heroes by the National Heroes Committee in 1995.
