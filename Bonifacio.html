         Andres Bonifacio

                                                    1863 - 1897

Andrés Bonifacio was the hero of the uprising in Balintawak in 1896. He was born in Tondo, Manila on November 30, 1863. From a young age, Bonifacio had great hopes and deep beliefs that his countrymen were capable of maintaining a free government. Driven by this conviction, he organized the Katipunan in Balintawak and led the call to arms against Spanish rule. He convinced Emilio Jacinto, known as the brain of the revolution, to join him in the cause. Together, they built a secret revolutionary society that would eventually set the entire country on fire with the spirit of independence.

When the secret of the Katipunan was exposed, Bonifacio and his men retreated to Balintawak. On August 26, the Katipunan tore their cedulas and began the revolt against Spanish rule. His favorite saying was "Liberty or Death," and the first revolt he led rang out with the cry of the Filipino people: "Long live the Philippine Republic!" However, a misunderstanding arose when a meeting was held at Tejeros, Malabon in 1877, where Bonifacio attempted to form a new government. These internal divisions would prove to be deeply damaging to the revolutionary movement he had built.

As the Spaniards learned more about the organization, Bonifacio was eventually arrested and tried by a court of war. He and his brother Procopio were sentenced to death on May 10, 1897. Despite his tragic end, Bonifacio's legacy to his motherland was democracy. He left his people lessons drawn from his Decalogue — to believe in God, to love their country and countrymen, and to understand that it is an honor to die for the cause of saving one's country from slavery. Andrés Bonifacio remains forever honored as the Father of the Revolution and the Founder of the Katipunan.

WATCH & LEARN



  • Andrés Bonifacio never attended formal school, yet he became one of the most well-read and influential revolutionary leaders in Philippine history.
  • He is commemorated with a national holiday — Bonifacio Day, observed every November 30, which also happens to be his birthday.
  • He married fellow revolutionary Gregoria de Jesús in 1893, who later became known as the "Lakambini of the Katipunan." Numerous streets, schools, and monuments across the country bear his name.
